Sinigang

Sinigang is a sour soup native to the Philippines. Sinigang means "stewed [dish]", it is a nominalized form of the Tagalog verb sigang, "to stew". The soup is usually accommodated by rice. It is one of the most well-known Filipino recipe!

Filipino Sinigang and Vietnamese Canh Chua

Canh chua, which means sour soup in Vietnamese, is the country's equivalent to Sinigang. A lot of the general flavor profile is pretty similar because it is made with the same tamarind base, but the Vietnamese version leans more toward the FISHY side than the PORK side.

Sinigang na Baboy

The Filipino dish sinigang na baboy consists of pork and taro cooked in a sour broth. Pork belly serves as the primary source of protein in this particular recipe. The taste of sinigang na baboy is sour. Pork ribs, veggies, and fresh tamarind or tamarind powder are used to make this sour soup. It is warming, delicious, and ideal for cold weather. It is also the most popular version of Sinigang.

Sinigang na Baka

A delicious version of sinigang is sinigang na baka, or beef sinigang. This meal has a lot of positive qualities. You'll enjoy how the sourness of the soup and the flavor of the beef blend together. It is fantastic. Fresh vegetables offer delicious flavors. You will also enjoy eating the tender beef by grabbing it using your hands, just like the way we eat BBQ ribs. It is amazing!

Sinigang na Bangus

Sinigang na Bangus or Milkfish in Sour Broth is an all-time favorite dish of many Filipinos. It is a dish made of vegetables boiled in sour broth, cooked until the vegetable turn glossy before adding the milkfish. The sour taste of tamarind juice gives the distinctive taste of the broth. One of the main ingredients is the milkfish.

Sinigang na Hipon

Sinigang na Hipon is a sour dish with shrimp as the main ingredient. This dish also contains vegetables like radish, snake beans, okra, and eggplant. This dish is best served in cold weather. It is best served hot with a cup of white rice!

Sinigang na Tilapia

Sinigang na Tilapia is a popular Filipino sour fish soup recipe. Since we pan-fried the tilapia beforehand, this version is tastier than the usual recipes. It adds an interesting flavor to the dish for some reason!

Sinigang na Kambing

Sinigang na Kambing is a sour soup made from unripe tamarind and goat meat. This dish is similar to sinigang and is ideal for eating during the colder months. This Sinigang na Kambing recipe uses goat meat, whereas traditional versions use the goat's head and feet. Food should not be wasted, which is why all parts of the animal should be consumed.

Sinigang na Manok

Sinigang na Manok is our final version of sinigang today. I know, it's quite surprising and unexpected to find Chicken Sinigang! Sinigang na Manok is a sour soup made with chicken, vegetables, and tamarind leaves. This recipe for chicken soup is similar to Sinigang. The only difference is that tamarind leaves are used. Some argue that Sinigang na Manok is simply another sinigang recipe. However, there are some noticeable differences that give each dish its own identity.

Sinigang

Sinigang is a Filipino soup or stew with a sour and savory flavor. It is most commonly associated with tamarind, but other sour fruits and leaves can be used as the souring agent.

Course: Main Course

Cuisine: Filipino

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour 40 minutes

Serving: 5 people

Calories: 222kcal per cup

Author: Ron Lawrence L. Comboy

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg pork belly/chicken/beef/pata/shrimp/goat/milkfish
  • 5 cups of water
  • 1 sachet Knorr Sinigang Mix
  • 2 pcs. tomato
  • 1 pc. onion
  • 3 pcs. long chili
  • 1 string of kangkong and string beans
  • 5 pcs. okra
  • 1 pc. eggplant
  • 1 tsp. fish sauce
  • 1 tsp. salt

Instructions

  1. Boil the pork/beef until it is tender.
  2. After the pork/beef has been boiled, add the vegetables one at a time.
  3. After you've finally added all the vegetables, add the souring agent, which is the Knorr sinigang mix
  4. Season with fish sauce, salt, and/or msg.
  5. Wait until all the vegetables are all cooked.
  6. Once cooked, serve the Sinigang with hot white rice, share and enjoy!
